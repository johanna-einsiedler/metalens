# Copyright Notice & Takedown (DMCA) — Metalens (TEMPLATE — not legal advice)

Metalens hosts files that users upload themselves and store privately. We respond to valid copyright complaints and, where required, remove or disable access to allegedly infringing material. Maintaining this process is part of what keeps the hosting safe harbor (US DMCA §512(c); EU eCommerce/DSA hosting safe harbor) available.

## To submit a takedown notice

Include, per §512(c)(3):

1. Your physical or electronic signature.
2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed.
3. Identification of the material to be removed and **enough information for us to locate it** (e.g. the public dataset URL / dataset id). Note: users' **private** files are not publicly accessible.
4. Your contact information.
5. A statement that you have a **good-faith belief** the use is not authorized.
6. A statement, **under penalty of perjury**, that the information is accurate and you are authorized to act for the rights holder.

## Our process

- On a valid notice we remove or disable access to the identified **public** material and notify the user.
- The user may submit a **counter-notice**; we follow the statutory restore timeline.
- We terminate repeat infringers' accounts.

Note: publishing on Metalens shares extracted **records**, not source PDFs, and is gated behind an explicit user rights-warranty (see TERMS.md). Private uploads are the user's own stored copy and are never redistributed.
